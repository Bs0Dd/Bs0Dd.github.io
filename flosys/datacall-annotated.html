The capture software (hack) I'm using puts a newline before every 0x03
byte which is the start of the encapsulated RLP frames (or whatever
they are)  I've tried to re-edit the dump to account for 0x03's that
occur within the frame.

Note we don't see the command going to the phone to setup the call.
I've also removed duplicate messages where possible.  Nothing was
sent to the terminal server at the other end other
than +++ prior to issuing ATH0

General form seems to be very similar to the frames used for
non-data calls (SMS, phonebook updates, what have you)

{03 - Header byte}
{20 - Length}
{02 - Command type}
{d9 - Sequence number (never seems to change) }
. RLP header (see below)
.
. RLP data 
.
.
{xx - CRC byte}

Emails from Cobus (22 February 1999) and Colin (25 June 1999) are worth
reading too as they provide some background to what happens on the 6110
at least.

* Update 9 Oct 1999 *
This looks to be the format of the first two bytes of the RLP frame,
it is a standard 16 bit header but bit reversed...

First Byte
D7  D6  D5  D4  D3  D2  D1  D0
NS4 NS3 NS2 NS1 NS0 S2  S1  C/R

Second Byte
D7  D6  D5  D4  D3  D2  D1  D0
NR5 NR4 NR3 NR2 NR1 NR0 P/F NS5
